  <div class="col-xs-12"><a class="anchor" id="intro"></a>
    <h2>Introduction</h2>
  </div>
</div>
<div class="row">
  <div class="col-xs-12">
    <p>
    The 7th International Workshop on Eye and Gaze in Computer Vision (GAZE 2026) at <a href="https://cvpr.thecvf.com/Conferences/2026" target="_blank">CVPR 2026</a> aims to encourage and highlight novel strategies for eye gaze estimation and prediction. The workshop topics include (but are not limited to):
    </p>
    <ul>
      <li>Foundation models and large-scale training for the eye and gaze.</li>
      <li>Gaze in egocentric vision, physical AI learning, and human–robot interaction.</li>
      <li>Understanding gaze in social interactions, human activities, and telepresence scenarios involving real or virtual agents and entities.</li>
      <li>Gaze estimation algorithms, including 3D gaze estimation, point-of-regard estimation, gaze following, gaze zone classification, etc.</li>
      <li>Detection and segmentation of the eye region, such as eye detection, pupil detection, eye-region landmark localization, etc.</li>
      <li>Human eye modeling and generation, including synthesis and animation from images or videos, etc.</li>
      <li>Eye gaze data collection, generation, and analysis, such as scanpath generation, etc.</li>
      <li>Applications of gaze tracking and analysis in real-world scenarios, including VR/AR, mobile devices, PCs, etc.</li>
    </ul>
    We will be hosting 2 invited speakers and will also be accepting the submission of full unpublished papers as done in previous versions of the workshop. These papers will be peer-reviewed via a double-blind process, and will be published in the official workshop proceedings and be presented at the workshop itself.
  </div>
</div> <br>
